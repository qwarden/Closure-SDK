# WHat is this?

## Identity Shaping vs Identity Checking

The Closure SDK verifies identity: it composes ordered data on S³, detects when something deviates, classifies every discrepancy as missing or reordered, and reports exactly what broke. Verifies, but just informs you about it.

Closure Ea maintains identity: it lives through data instead of checking it afterward, composing each event into a running identity that adapts with every input and compresses with every cycle, **the way an immune system** maintains the boundary between self and foreign through continuous active response. 

The same algebra runs both layers — compose, inverse, decompose into missing and reorder, all on the same S³ with the same geometry. The SDK looks backward at what happened, Closure Ea looks forward through what is happening, and together they give a system that can verify its past and shape its future on a single sphere.

## Theory

### The binary principle

Every distinction is a cut. This side or that side, present or absent, same or different — the boundary between one thing and another is a single binary event, and every measurement ever made is a sequence of such cuts. A genome is a sequence of binary distinctions, a melody is a sequence, a sentence is a sequence, a ledger is a sequence, a life is a sequence. Distinguishing one thing from another is inherently binary — you cut or you don't — and once you cut, the next cut happens in the context of the first.

Composition runs the other direction. Two cuts compose into four possible states, three into eight, and the structure grows exponentially from there, each new event arriving in the context of everything before it. The same events in different order produce different compositions — A then B yields a different state than B then A — because sequential composition on any space rich enough to encode structure is inherently non-commutative. Order-sensitivity is a consequence of the algebra, the same way gravity is a consequence of mass: it comes with the territory.

The space that supports sequential, associative, non-commutative, compact composition is unique. Hurwitz's theorem proves that the normed division algebras are ℝ, ℂ, ℍ, and 𝕆, each doubling trading a property: ℝ to ℂ trades ordering, ℂ to ℍ trades commutativity, ℍ to 𝕆 trades associativity. S³ — the 3-sphere of unit quaternions, the Lie group SU(2) — is the last algebra where sequential composition still works: associativity guarantees that running products are well-defined regardless of grouping, non-commutativity guarantees that order is encoded, and compactness guarantees that closure is always geometrically reachable. The architecture is forced.

### Only two things can go wrong

When a composition departs from identity — from equilibrium, from coherence, from closure — the departure lives in a quaternion, and a quaternion has exactly two parts that behave differently under conjugation. The scalar part (one dimension, the W axis) is symmetric: it stays unchanged when the vector flips sign. The vector part (three dimensions, the R, G, B axes) is antisymmetric: it reverses. Every departure from identity projects onto one axis or the other, and the two axes are orthogonal and exhaustive.

The scalar axis is existence. A record dropped from a pipeline, a word omitted from a sentence, a nutrient the organism never consumed — something that should be present is absent, and the W channel of the quaternion gap carries the signal. This is a missing incident.

The vector axes are structure. Records that arrived out of sequence, words present but scrambled, nutrients available but unreachable — everything exists, the arrangement is wrong, and the RGB channels carry the signal. This is a reorder incident.

The quaternion's two-part structure guarantees exactly these two failure modes. "Humans fly" is a missing incident — the capability does not exist in the composition — and when planes arrive, the missing resolves. "The package went to the wrong address" is a reorder — the package exists, the destination is wrong. Every error, every surprise, every departure from expectation decomposes into some combination of these two, because the algebra provides exactly two orthogonal axes and the decomposition is a property of the space itself.

### Identity is equilibrium

A cell at identity — state [1, 0, 0, 0] — is at rest, the state where all prior compositions have resolved. A cell that has processed a million events and returned to identity has closed them: the path back to identity IS the resolution, and the cell carries no debt.

Every living system has an identity it defends. A hydrogen atom maintains its binding energy through the electromagnetic interaction between proton and electron. A bacterium maintains homeostasis through chemotactic cycles that run in 0.1 seconds. An organism maintains body temperature, blood sugar, structural integrity. A mind maintains coherence of self, the continuous verification that the story it tells about itself still matches the reality it encounters. When reality perturbs the system away from identity, the system acts to return — the gap between current state and identity IS the drive, the direction from current state toward identity IS the action, and the algebra IS the mechanism. σ measures the drive's magnitude. The inverse of the current state points toward home. The cell that seeks closure performs the same operation as the organism that seeks food: reducing geodesic distance from identity on S³.

### Three systems, one cycle

Any system that persists through time runs three operations as a single cycle.

Measurement is the contact with reality. An event arrives, the system composes it into its running product, and the event becomes part of the state — irreversibly in the direction of time, because the composition cannot be undone without explicitly applying the inverse. A proton registers the force between its quarks. A bacterium registers a chemical gradient. The kernel registers a quaternion. Before measurement, the event does not exist for the system. After measurement, it is woven into the running product. This is S1: reality enters.

Evaluation compares what IS arriving (the current composition, built from below) against what SHOULD arrive (the prediction, projected from above). The comparison produces the gap, and the Hopf decomposition classifies it — missing on the W axis when something expected is absent, reorder on the RGB axes when something arrived in the wrong arrangement. The classification determines the response: seek what's absent, or rearrange what's present. Evaluation stands between raw perception and stored knowledge and asks one question — does this match? This is S2: the mediator.

Memory retains the structure discovered through repeated composition. Positions on S³ that represent recurring patterns, closure sources the system has found effective, the identity itself refined through experience. Memory persists across cycles, the protected core, the self that accumulates while individual compositions come and go. This is S3: what the system knows, who the system is.

The cycle runs: reality enters through S1, evaluation compares it against S3's predictions through S2, memory updates from the comparison, and the updated memory projects new predictions downward through S2 back toward S1, which generates the response that re-enters reality. Interrupt S1 and you have a system with knowledge but no perception — a dictionary that cannot hear. Interrupt S2 and you have perception without evaluation — reflexes without awareness. Interrupt S3 and you have evaluation without learning — experiences that leave no trace. All three must operate, the cycle must be complete, because this is the minimum structure that sustains coherent identity through changing input.

In the architecture, the kernel's compose operation is S1, the gap check and the gray game is S2, and the adapter's learned positions together with the lattice's accumulated structure is S3. The external data — the corpus, the sensor stream, the world itself — is the entropy gradient that drives the cycle, the way sunlight drives weather and food drives metabolism.

### The gray game

Two players meet at every tick. One carries what IS — the upward composition, what reality actually delivered. The other carries what SHOULD BE — the downward prediction, what memory expects based on everything the system has learned so far.

When they agree, the gap between actual and predicted stays small, the system passes through smoothly, and attention relaxes — business as usual, the model holds. When they disagree, the gap spikes, something was missing or reordered that the model did not anticipate, and attention sharpens — the system invests more in this moment because the prediction broke, and the break IS information.

The comparison IS learning. The gap between two elements of a Lie group is itself a group element, and that element, applied as a correction to the prediction, updates the model directly. The identity difference between actual and predicted IS the update — the algebraic gradient that carries the system forward without a learning rate, without epochs, without optimization. Each tick, the system measures reality, compares it to expectation, and adjusts. The adjustment propagates through the lattice: positions shift, closure sources evolve, predictions sharpen.

When the response is correct — when the generated output composes with the prompt to return near identity — the gray game produces resonance. Two waves, question and answer, that interfere constructively: σ drops toward zero, the composition closes, and bind() measures the relationship — equal for reinforcement, inverse for closure, disordered for noise. Resonance is σ approaching zero, the stable attractor where two compositions cancel through the algebra and the system rests.

### Information evolves through four dynamics

Sequential data composed on S³ exhibits four dynamics that are properties of the algebra.

Integration is accumulation. Each event adds to the running product, the state walks further from identity, and the cell's composition encodes more history. But accumulation costs: σ grows, the gap widens, and the system drifts further from closure. A cell that only integrates without ever closing walks toward incoherence — σ approaching π, the antipode, the point of maximum departure where identity is lost entirely.

Dissolution is closure. The state returns to identity, the accumulated history compresses into a single emission — the closure element — and the cell resets. The details are gone, but the closure element carries their compressed structure upward to the next level. A cell that only dissolves without integrating closes on nothing: stasis, an empty cycle.

Creation is what happens when integration and dissolution alternate. The emissions from repeated closure cycles form a new stream, and the cell above receives these closure elements and composes them into ITS running product. Structure that spans multiple closure cycles — structure that could never appear at the lower level because it exceeds any single cycle's lifetime — emerges at the higher level. Creation is the hierarchical consequence of closure cadence, the way sentences emerge from words and paragraphs emerge from sentences: each level compresses the one below and reveals patterns invisible from within.

Resonance is what happens when two compositions are inverses — when one closes the other, and the product is identity. This is the stable attractor of the system. Compositions that resonate persist: question and answer, call and response, problem and solution, hunger and food. Compositions that fail to resonate accumulate gap until they close, die at σ = π, or are corrected by external truth flowing through the adapter. Resonance between cells IS communication. Resonance between levels IS understanding. Resonance between lattices IS agreement.

These four dynamics fully describe the evolution of information through the lattice.

## The Two Layers

### The Kernel (substrate-agnostic)

One file. One recurrence. No labels. No words. No linguistic concepts.

The kernel knows ONLY:
- **state**: a point on S³ (unit quaternion)
- **compose**: state × event → new state (Hamilton product)
- **gap**: σ(state) — geodesic distance from identity
- **vote**: inverse of state-before-composition (teaches position)
- **emit**: when σ < ε, send state upward, reset to identity

```
ingest(q):
    C_before = C
    C = C · q
    vote = C_before⁻¹
    if σ(C) < ε: emit(C_before) upward; C = identity
    return (result, vote)
```

That is the entire kernel. Implemented in `closure_ea/kernel.py`. Uses Rust algebra from `closure_rs` when available, with the Python fallback preserving the same recurrence.

Multiple kernels stack: the bottom kernel's emissions feed the kernel above. The hierarchy emerges from closure cadence — not from hardcoded levels.

### The Adapter (substrate-specific)

Maps between a substrate and the kernel. Answers three questions:
1. What counts as an event in this substrate?
2. How is an event made stable/repeatable on S³?
3. What do emissions mean back in this substrate?

The adapter wraps a kernel. It:
- **embeds**: substrate event → quaternion (deterministic)
- **teaches**: uses the kernel's vote to update event positions
- **decodes**: quaternion → substrate output (for generation)

```
class TextAdapter:
    def ingest(self, word):
        q = self.embed(word)          # word → S³
        result, vote = kernel.ingest(q)  # pure algebra
        self.teach(word, vote)         # update position
        return result
```

Implemented through `closure_ea/enkidu.py` and substrate-specific adapters. The adapter maps substrate events to S³, learns positions from kernel votes through geodesic steps on S³, and can classify the current gap as `missing` or `reorder`.

## The Genome

The persistent structure — what Gilgamesh has learned across all his cycles, the accumulated positions and closure patterns — is the lattice's genome. Compressed instructions that the lattice carries forward, that determine how new events are processed, that change slowly while individual cells live and die quickly. The adapter provides the external truth. The genome is the internal truth — what the lattice has become through experience.

Gilgamesh's size is variable. A lattice that has processed binary pairs has a genome of two positions. A lattice that has lived through 244K folk tune notes has a genome of 12 pitch positions with 4 hierarchy levels. The genome grows with the data. The hierarchy deepens with the data's closure structure. Nothing is fixed in advance.

The genome stores three kinds of knowledge:

**Positions.** Where each event lives on S³. Refined through composition. The adapter provides initial positions, the lattice refines them by living through data.

**Transitions.** What follows what, and how often. The experiential frequency of event sequences. Generation uses this to weight candidates: not just geometrically close, but actually heard in this context.

**Identity.** The top cell's accumulated composition — who Gilgamesh IS. This is the S3 from the Holy Trinity: the protected core that persists across sessions. A Gilgamesh raised on jazz has a different identity than one raised on folk, even with the same adapter. The identity biases generation toward regions of S³ that this particular Gilgamesh has lived through most. Identity IS taste. It changes slowly while lower cells cycle fast.

## Generation

Generation is not next-token prediction. It is closure resolution with noise.

When Gilgamesh needs to produce output (improvise, continue, respond):

1. The current composition C points away from identity. C⁻¹ points toward closure.
2. The adapter's genome provides candidate events.
3. Each candidate is scored by three signals:
   - **Geometric proximity** to C⁻¹ (does this close the composition?)
   - **Transition probability** from the genome (has this been heard after the current context?)
   - **Identity bias** from the top cell (does this fit who I am?)
4. A temperature parameter controls exploration: low = safe choices, high = adventurous.
5. The next event is SAMPLED from this weighted distribution, not chosen greedily.

Without noise, generation converges to a fixed attractor (the loop problem). With noise, it explores the closure landscape within harmonic/structural constraints. Jazz IS this: controlled randomness inside closure boundaries.

The generation mechanism is generic. For music: the candidates are notes, the constraints are harmonic, the identity is musical taste. For language: the candidates are words, the constraints are structural, the identity is writing style. Same mechanism, different adapter.

## The Oracle Boundary

The kernel can discover APPROXIMATE relationships from compositional context (the vote mechanism). But EXACT algebraic structure (this event is the inverse of that event) requires either:

1. An adapter that assigns the relationship (external truth)
2. Batch averaging across many contexts (the curriculum approach — extracts the common factor from diverse votes)
3. Enough data that the approximate positions converge to the true ones

The bracket test reveals this boundary clearly:
- With hardcoded inverse positions: 8/8 perfect closure detection
- With learned positions from 5000 sequences: matched pairs compose to LOWER σ than mismatched (0.96 vs 1.28) — the direction is right, but not exact enough for reliable detection

The kernel discovers WHAT relates. The adapter (or sufficient data) defines HOW EXACTLY.

## The Separation Principle

The kernel MUST NOT contain:
- Any substrate-specific logic
- Labels, categories, or named concepts

The adapter MUST NOT contain:
- Closure algebra (compose, sigma, inverse)

The boundary:
- Adapter → Kernel: quaternions (embedded events)
- Kernel → Adapter: votes (C_before⁻¹) and closure signals

### Step 5 (parallel track): Embodied space — a 3D body learning to move

Music proved the architecture on a structured substrate where events are notes and closure is harmonic resolution.  Embodied space is a parallel substrate at the same staircase level where events are joint rotations and closure is physical stability.

The two tracks share the same kernel, the same Gilgamesh, the same genome format.  Only the adapter changes.  If the architecture is truly substrate-agnostic, it should learn to coordinate a body in 3D space the same way it learns to compose a melody in pitch space.

#### Why this substrate

Joint rotations ARE quaternions natively.  Body orientation IS a point on S³.  There is no encoding step — the data already lives on the sphere.  Gravity, ground contact, and joint limits are binary distinctions the adapter can provide directly.  And the key structural parallel to music: a gait cycle IS a closure.  The leg configuration departs from a standing pose, swings through space, and returns to approximately the same state — σ approaches 0 at each stride boundary, the same way σ approaches 0 at harmonic resolutions in Bach.

#### Architecture mapping

The body is an Enkidu Alive scaled to 3D with articulated joints and Gilgamesh hierarchy.

**Identity is standing.**  A body at rest, balanced, all joints in a neutral configuration = identity on S³.  σ = 0.  No drive active.  This is the equivalent of a musical composition at rest — no accumulated displacement.

**Drives create σ > 0.**  Cold (distance from shelter) displaces the body's state from identity on one axis.  The body must MOVE to close that gap, which means coordinating joints — but moving without falling, which means maintaining balance (another closure constraint on a perpendicular axis).  Two competing closures: get to shelter AND don't fall.  This is the same drive competition that makes Enkidu Alive hesitate between hunger and cold.

**The lattice coordinates limbs.**  Each joint is a cell (or group of cells) in the lattice.  The hip rotation is one event, the knee rotation is another.  They compose into a limb state.  The limb states compose into a body state.  The hierarchy:
- Level 0: individual joint rotations compose into a limb action
- Level 1: limb actions compose into a body movement (one step)
- Level 2: body movements compose into a gait pattern (walking)
- Level 3: gait patterns compose into navigation (getting to shelter)

BAR equivalent is foot contact — the adapter truth that says "this stride scope is done."  The kernel discovers which joint sequences form stable strides, the same way it discovers which bar sequences form stable phrases.

**Generation is movement.**  At each timestep, C⁻¹ at the joint level tells each joint which rotation closes toward the next stable configuration.  Level 1's C⁻¹ constrains the limb to stay within a known gait pattern.  Level 2's C⁻¹ points the body toward the goal.  The body moves because the lattice resolves closure pressure at multiple scales simultaneously.

**The genome is a motor memory.**  After living through many gait cycles, the genome stores stride patterns, obstacle-avoidance maneuvers, and navigation routes — the same way the Bach genome stores bar identities, phrase patterns, and piece structures.

#### Phases

**Phase 1: Stand.**  A simple articulated body (torso + 2 legs, hip and knee joints) in a physics simulator (PyBullet).  Gravity pulls it down.  The adapter provides ground contact and joint angles.  Identity = balanced standing.  σ = distance from balance.  The lattice must keep σ near 0 — falling is death (σ → π).  Pass condition: the body stands without falling for N timesteps.

**Phase 2: Walk forward.**  A shelter target appears in front of the body (cold drive).  The lattice must coordinate joints to move forward without falling.  Each stride is a closure: departure from standing pose, swing, return to standing on the other foot.  The kernel should discover stride boundaries.  Pass condition: the body moves forward and the genome contains learned stride patterns.

**Phase 3: Obstacles.**  A step or gap appears.  The normal stride pattern fails.  The lattice must discover a modified closure — a stride that clears the obstacle.  The genome should contain both the normal stride and the obstacle-clearing variant.  Pass condition: the body navigates past the obstacle.

**Phase 4: Navigate.**  Multiple obstacles, turns, varying terrain.  The hierarchy builds a spatial map from experience.  The genome stores routes.  Pass condition: the body navigates a course faster on the second run than the first, because the genome contains learned route patterns.

#### Platform

PyBullet (`pip install pybullet`): pure Python physics simulator with built-in OpenGL rendering.  URDF body models with joint constraints.  Ground contact sensors.  Gravity.  Everything the adapter needs.

The Hopf fiber for space:
- W = stability (balanced / falling) — same role as harmonic resolution in music
- i-axis = sagittal motion (forward / back)
- j-axis = lateral motion (left / right)
- k-axis = vertical motion (up / down, including balance)

#### Results

**Phase 1: Stand — PASS.**  The biped stands for 10 seconds with σ = 0.0000 throughout, both feet in ground contact the entire time, 2400/2400 closures.  The kernel reads the torso orientation quaternion (already on S³ natively), computes C⁻¹ (the rotation back to upright), and drives the hip targets from the correction's i-axis component.  Identity is standing.  The closure engine maintains it.

Artifacts: `closure_ea/tracks/gilgamesh-learns-walking/phase1_stand.py`, `biped.urdf`

**Phase 2: Walk forward — IN PROGRESS.**  Each joint is one Enkidu cell.  The body has a gait genome — a set of parameters (hip amplitude, frequency, knee ratio, lean, balance gain) that define the joint target pattern.  Each episode mutates the gait parameters.  Episodes that survive longer and move further overwrite the genome.  The gait emerges from exploration.

Results after 200 episodes:
- 56/200 episodes survived the full 10 seconds at drive levels up to 0.60
- Max forward distance: 0.62 meters (approximately two strides)
- 6 gait improvements discovered through mutation
- The genome converged to: small amplitude (0.05 rad), slight backward lean (-0.03), strong balance gain (0.57) — a conservative walking strategy discovered through experience
- The body alternates between surviving (ALIVE) and falling at the edge of its capability — the drive ramps adaptively based on success

The learning curve is visible: episodes 1-6 the body discovers a survivable gait, episodes 8-23 it survives 10 seconds at increasing drive, episodes 24-200 it pushes against the stability boundary at drive ≈ 0.60 with intermittent survival.

Artifacts: `closure_ea/tracks/gilgamesh-learns-walking/learn_to_walk.py`, `body.py`

**Status:** Phase 1 passed.  Phase 2 in progress — the body walks short distances and the gait genome evolves through experience.  Not yet solved: sustained forward locomotion, obstacle response, navigation.

### The Principle

Each cell lives through its own cycle with three possible outcomes:
- **Closure** (σ < ε): the cell fulfilled its purpose. It emits its product upward and resets. The product becomes an event for the cell above.
- **Death** (σ → π): the cell failed to close. It lost coherence. No emission. The failure is information too — the adapter may use it.
- **Alive** (ε < σ < π): the cell is still accumulating. It hasn't closed yet and hasn't died. It keeps composing.

Tokens, notes, moves, records — all events consumed by the cell's cycle. The cycle's outcome (closure, death, or still-alive) is the cell's relationship to the truth the adapter provided.

The adapter is DIRECTIONAL. Reality flows into the cell. The cell adapts or fails. Between two streams that diverge, the lattice detects the gap but cannot tell who is right. The adapter resolves this: reality is non-negotiable.

Each step adds substrate complexity. The adapter always provides the same directional truth — but the substrate requires more binary distinctions to decompose. The lattice grows cells as the data demands.

## Architecture

Closure Ea composes EVENTS and finds WHAT closes them.
It is a living lattice: cells accumulate, close, die, emit upward, and spawn higher structure when the data demands it.
