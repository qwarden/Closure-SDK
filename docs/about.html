About

Psychology studies the mind. The mind is made of memories. The brain stores memories as information. Information is real, and real things have a shape. That shape is what I have spent years studying, and what this project is modeling.

The shape turns out to be S³ — the unit 3-sphere of quaternions. Every brain state is a point on it. Every update is a Hamilton product. When a state drifts far enough from what the genome predicts — past the BKT phase boundary at geodesic distance π/4 — the brain closes: it learns, corrects, and moves on. One arithmetic operation. No gradient descent. No attention weights. Convergence is a fixed-point theorem on the sphere.

I'm a psychology student and independent researcher in information geometry, with published work in neuroscience. This is the architecture I've been building toward: a complete cognitive system derived from first principles, where the geometry itself produces attention, memory, learning, and affect — not as separate modules, but as consequences of the same Hamilton product running at every layer.
