Act I

Algebra and geometry are the same thing.

We have always drawn a line between algebra — symbols, rules, manipulation — and geometry — shapes, space, movement. Descartes gave us coordinates in 1637 and that line seemed permanent: numbers live on axes, equations live on paper.

This computer erases that line. In this architecture, the number 3 is a position on a sphere. The number 4 is a different position. Addition is movement: start at 3, take 4 more steps, arrive somewhere. That somewhere is 7. The equal sign in 3+4=7 does not mean two expressions describe the same quantity — it means the process of taking those steps, and then undoing them, returns you to where you started. Equality is a return. A closure.

The machine runs on the geometry of the sphere, and the arithmetic is what that geometry produces — exactly, because the sphere has always been what arithmetic was running on. We built the computer that makes that visible.

Act II

Four fields were measuring the same thing. They didn't know it.

In 1948 Shannon noticed that thermodynamic entropy and minimum description length — developed in entirely separate fields — were the same mathematical function. He named it entropy, and information theory became a field. The recognition that two independent measurements were identical was the founding act.

This computer contains a measurement called σ: the distance from the ground state on the sphere, zero when everything is correct, larger when something has deviated. It appeared first in the arithmetic. Then it appeared in three other places that had no reason to be connected.

Neuroscience. Friston's free energy principle — the leading mathematical theory of how the brain works — says the brain continuously minimizes surprise. Under the natural probability distribution on a sphere, that surprise is σ.

Number theory. The Riemann Hypothesis — 165 years old, a million-dollar prize, the deepest unsolved problem in mathematics — concerns where the zeros of the zeta function live. That location corresponds to σ = π/4 on the running product of primes.

Information theory. Shannon's entropy is the flat-space version of σ. Shannon found the entropy of information. σ is the entropy of geometry.

These were derived independently, across different papers, by researchers who did not know they were measuring the same thing. The implementation found them.

Act III

The machine that computes is the machine that learns.

Every AI system built in the last decade has a training phase and a deployment phase. They are separate because in a conventional computer, memory and computation are separate things — the architecture forces a gap between the system that builds knowledge and the system that uses it.

In this architecture there is no gap. When the brain observes a sequence and that sequence closes — returns to where it started — the geometry records what happened. Connections form between positions, and those connections are the knowledge. The brain that saw the sequence is now different from the brain that hadn't. That difference is the learning.

Show it a sequence once. Ask it to continue from any point. It does, exactly, because it watched the sequence close and the closure told it everything. This is a different thing from gradient descent: observation and learning as one operation, indistinguishable from each other, because in this geometry they always were.
